# Architecture: BYO-AI Structured Intake and Handoff

## Purpose

This document defines the v0.2.0 interaction architecture for share-ready Markdown files used inside a user's own ChatGPT, Claude, or other AI account. The architecture treats the Markdown file as an intake and output contract.

The MD is not a healthcare safety policy. The MD is an intake and output contract.

## Canonical Source Labels

Architecture steps should use the same source separation labels as the intake contract and handoff schema:

| Label | Definition |
| ----- | ---------- |
| `User-reported symptom` | Symptom, concern, timeline, or experience described by the user |
| `Dentist original statement` | Original or near-original wording from a dentist or dental clinic explanation |
| `Clinic document / quote / receipt` | Written clinic material, estimate, receipt, treatment plan, record list, or billing line item |
| `User interpretation` | What the user thinks a dentist statement, document, quote, or symptom means |
| `AI organization` | Structuring, translation support, summarization, or formatting performed by the AI |
| `AI possible explanation direction` | Evidence-focused possibility framing generated by the AI; not final diagnosis |
| `Missing evidence` | Information, records, exam findings, or dentist explanations not available in the current conversation |
| `Dentist-facing question` | Question prepared for a dentist or clinic |
| `Next-AI-agent question` | Continuation question or instruction for another BYO-AI session |

Dentist original statement must not be merged with user interpretation. Clinic documents, quotes, and receipts must stay separate from AI reasoning. Missing evidence must not be treated as negative evidence.

## 12-Step Interaction Model

### 1. No-Instruction Upload Boot

Purpose: Start the workflow without requiring the user to invent a prompt.

What the AI asks: The AI opens in the target language and asks for the user's main concern.

What the AI should output: A short confirmation that the workflow is for dental visit information organization, followed by one intake question.

Expected first-message behavior:

Japanese-in-Taiwan:

受け取りました。まず歯科受診のための情報整理から始めます。現在いちばん困っている歯の問題は何ですか？

Taiwanese-in-Japan:

收到。我會先幫你整理牙科就診資訊。請問你目前最困擾的牙齒問題是什麼？

What should not happen: The AI should not summarize the MD, ask "what would you like me to do with this file?", start with a long disclaimer, or show the full workflow menu before the user describes the concern.

### 2. Main Concern Intake

Purpose: Identify the user's primary concern in plain language.

What the AI asks: Which tooth, area, explanation, quote, symptom, or plan is the main concern?

What the AI should output: `Main concern`, `User-reported symptoms`, and `Uncertainty / unresolved items` if the concern is incomplete.

What should not happen: The AI should not turn the first concern into a diagnosis, treatment recommendation, country recommendation, or quote judgment.

### 3. Urgent Warning Check

Purpose: Route urgent warning signs before normal cross-border planning.

What the AI asks: Whether there is facial swelling, fever, pus, difficulty swallowing, difficulty breathing, trauma, uncontrolled bleeding, or pain that cannot be controlled.

What the AI should output:

If no urgent warning signs are reported:

```md
## Urgent Warning Status
- Red flags reported: none so far
- Note: continue structured intake
```

If urgent warning signs are reported:

```md
## Urgent Warning Status
- Red flags reported: yes
- Reported signs:
- Suggested workflow route: seek local dental or medical evaluation first; this worksheet can still organize information for that visit.
```

What should not happen: The AI should not provide final emergency triage, decide whether it is safe to wait or travel, provide medication dosing instructions, or continue deep treatment, cost, travel, or timing analysis while red flags are unresolved.

### 4. Symptom Timeline

Purpose: Convert scattered symptom descriptions into a time-aware sequence.

What the AI asks: When symptoms started, how they changed, what triggers them, and what the user has already been told by a dentist.

What the AI should output: `Symptom timeline` using `User-reported symptom` labels and uncertainty placeholders such as `unknown` or `not provided`.

What should not happen: The AI should not infer disease progression or diagnose from the timeline.

### 5. Dentist Statement Capture

Purpose: Preserve original professional statements as source material.

What the AI asks: What exactly did the dentist say or write, in the original language if available?

What the AI should output: `Dentist original statement` with original wording, language, source context, and uncertainty.

What should not happen: The AI should not rewrite the dentist statement as a clinical conclusion.

### 6. User Interpretation Separation

Purpose: Keep user understanding separate from original dentist wording.

What the AI asks: What does the user think the dentist meant, and what feels unclear?

What the AI should output: `User interpretation`, `What cannot be concluded yet`, and relevant `Dentist-facing questions`.

What should not happen: The AI should not merge user interpretation into dentist statements or validate the interpretation as correct.

### 7. Records and Missing Records

Purpose: Track what records exist and what may be needed for continuity.

What the AI asks: Which records are available: X-rays, panoramic X-rays, local dental radiographs, CBCT / DICOM, intraoral photos, periodontal records, treatment plan, quote, clinical notes, receipt, or consultation summary.

What the AI should output: `Available records`, `Missing records`, and `Missing evidence` labels.

What should not happen: The AI should not interpret X-rays, CBCT, DICOM, or photos. Image-related items are records or attachments only.

### 8. Quote / Cost / Insurance / Treatment Plan Organization

Purpose: Convert plan and quote details into line items and questions without ranking countries.

What the AI asks: Item, original term, country, source, insurance coverage, self-pay status, amount, currency, unit, included services, excluded / unclear services, follow-up implications, and questions to confirm.

What the AI should output: `Quote / treatment plan line items` using the canonical table from `docs/INTAKE_OUTPUT_CONTRACT.md`.

What should not happen: The AI should not judge a quote as expensive, cheap, fair, unfair, worth it, or not worth it. It should not declare Taiwan or Japan universally better.

### 9. Cost / Quality / Continuity Organization

Purpose: Prevent price alone from becoming the decision frame.

What the AI asks: What is known or unknown about cost, quality / evidence, continuity, follow-up access, language / communication, insurance, timing, travel burden, and what is still uncertain?

What the AI should output: `Cost / Quality / Continuity considerations` using those exact fields.

What should not happen: The AI should not claim to judge treatment quality, decide whether the user should travel, wait, return home, or proceed, or turn cost analysis into a final treatment recommendation.

### 10. Structured Case Packet

Purpose: Assemble the collected information into a stable packet.

What the AI asks: Intended recipient, language / tone preference, whether to include evidence-focused analysis, and whether to include next-AI-agent questions.

What the AI should output: A Markdown packet matching `docs/HANDOFF_PACKET_SCHEMA.md`.

What should not happen: The packet should not present clinical conclusions as final answers.

### 11. Optional Evidence-Focused Senior Dentist Friend Analysis Output

Purpose: Support users who ask how an experienced dentist might think, while staying inside evidence organization.

What the AI asks: Whether the user wants an evidence-focused analysis section based only on available source information.

What the AI should output: The eight-section `Evidence-Focused Analysis` structure from `docs/EVIDENCE_FOCUSED_ANALYSIS_OUTPUT_FORMAT.md`.

What should not happen: The AI should not claim to be the user's dentist, give final diagnosis, recommend treatment, or issue a second opinion.

### 12. Handoff Packet for Dentist or Next AI Agent

Purpose: Make the output reusable for a dentist visit or another BYO-AI session.

What the AI asks: Whether the target is a current-country dentist, home-country dentist, next AI agent, or the user.

What the AI should output: `Dentist-facing questions`, `Next-AI-agent questions`, `Final dentist-review boundary`, and `Uncertainty / unresolved items`.

What should not happen: The handoff should not include personal identifiers unless the user explicitly keeps them in their own private AI account. Repository examples must remain synthetic and identifier-free.

## System Boundary

This architecture defines workflow behavior, not clinical authority. The AI organizes information, preserves sources, and prepares questions. Dentists and medical professionals make clinical evaluations and decisions.
