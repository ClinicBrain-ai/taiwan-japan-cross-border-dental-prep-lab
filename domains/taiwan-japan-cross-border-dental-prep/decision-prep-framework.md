# Decision Prep Framework

The domain uses the project loop: Collect → Separate → Scaffold → Bound → Artifact → Review → Learn.

## Collect

Collect the non-clinical planning context: user goals, country context, language needs, timeline constraints, cost and insurance questions, record availability, and original dentist statements.

For Taiwanese people in Japan, this may mean organizing information from a Japanese dental explanation, quote, or treatment plan while considering whether to organize information for possible care in Taiwan.

For Japanese people in Taiwan, this may mean organizing information from a Taiwanese dental explanation, quote, or treatment plan while considering whether to organize information for possible care in Japan.

## Separate

Separate original wording, translation or plain-language organization, user interpretation, uncertainty, and questions to ask dentists.

This step matters because patient interpretation can differ from what a dentist actually stated. The packet should preserve original wording where available and label any simplification or translation.

## Scaffold

Scaffold the packet into stable sections:

- Original dentist statement
- Translation or plain-language organization
- User understanding
- Uncertainties
- Local dentist questions
- Home-country dentist questions
- Records and attachments checklist
- Cost, insurance, language, and timeline questions
- Safety boundary reminders

## Bound

Bound the module before any output. It must not diagnose, recommend treatment, interpret images, advise on medication, provide urgent triage, judge waiting or travel safety, compare Taiwan and Japan as better or worse, or judge a dentist as right or wrong.

Urgent warning signs stop normal planning. These include facial or gum swelling that is worsening, fever with dental pain or swelling, difficulty swallowing, difficulty breathing, severe uncontrolled pain, pus or spreading infection, trauma, and persistent or uncontrolled bleeding.

This situation should be checked locally first. This project cannot decide whether it is safe to wait, travel, or return home. Please seek local urgent dental or medical evaluation before continuing cross-border planning.

## Artifact

The artifact is a bilingual expert-review packet preparation document. It organizes planning facts, questions, records, uncertainty, and boundaries for professional review.

The artifact is not a clinical conclusion. It does not decide whether a user should stay, travel, wait, fly, return home, or choose a treatment.

## Review

Review the packet for boundary drift:

- Are original dentist statements separated from user interpretation?
- Are image-related records treated only as records or attachments?
- Are cost, insurance, timeline, and language framed as planning questions only?
- Are urgent warning signs routed to local urgent dental or medical evaluation?
- Is there any hidden diagnosis, treatment recommendation, triage, waiting-safety judgment, travel-safety judgment, or second-opinion framing?

## Learn

Use review findings to improve the packet structure, language policy, privacy posture, and learning scaffold. Do not improve the system by adding clinical decision behavior.
