# Learning Objectives

This domain module is a learning scaffold for non-experts and builders studying AI-native professional learning systems.

Learners should understand:

- Why cross-border dental care planning is complex.
- Why dentist statements must be separated from patient interpretation.
- Why records matter for continuity of care.
- Why urgent warning signs must stop normal planning.
- Why cost, insurance, timeline, and language are planning factors only.
- Why final decisions require dentist or medical professional evaluation.
- How decision preparation differs from decision making.

## Domain complexity

Cross-border planning can combine language differences, unfamiliar dental terminology, cost and insurance uncertainty, treatment staging and follow-up needs, continuity of care across countries, record transfer, travel and timeline constraints, and differences between dentist statements and patient interpretation.

The learning goal is not to make a dental decision. The learning goal is to organize information so a dentist can review it more efficiently.

## Separation skill

A good packet separates:

- Original wording from the dentist
- Translation or plain-language organization
- User interpretation
- Uncertainty
- Questions to ask dentists

This separation reduces accidental overclaiming. It makes clear what came from a dentist, what was translated or simplified, what the user believes, and what still needs professional review.

## Records and continuity

Learners should understand why records can matter for continuity of care: X-rays, panoramic X-rays, local dental radiographs, CBCT / DICOM if already available, intraoral photos if already available, periodontal records, treatment plan, quote, and clinical notes or consultation summary.

Image-related records are records or attachments only. This module does not teach image interpretation.
